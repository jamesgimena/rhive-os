### Bld - 16
# Building Condition Summary: Building 16

**Inspector:** Senior Project Manager, RHive Construction  
**Inspection Target:** Building 16 Eaves, Gutters, and Lower Roof Edge  

### Video Audio Transcription
*"Building 16 has eave intake, and gutters are installed by nail."*

### Defects Identified
1. **Incorrect Flashing/Underlayment Sequence:** The Ice & Water shield is installed improperly in relation to the eave drip edge, creating a severe trap for water intrusion.
2. **Outdated Gutter Fasteners:** The gutters are secured using traditional gutter nails/spikes rather than modern hidden hangers. Over time, these spikes back out due to thermal expansion and ice accumulation, leading to sagging gutters, fascia rot, and water backing up into the eaves.

### CRITICAL: Drip Edge & Ice/Water Shield Analysis
Based on the video inspection, the Ice & Water shield is located **UNDER** the metal drip edge. 

* **Video Citation:** At the 0:06 – 0:09 mark, as the camera pans directly over the gutter viewing the roof edge, the bottom flange of the metal drip edge is visibly resting *on top* of the dark membrane (Ice & Water shield). 
* **Impact:** As discussed in the HOA management call, this improper sequence is a major building defect. Instead of the Ice & Water shield overlapping the metal to shed water into the gutter, water that gets under the shingles hits the metal and is channeled backwards or underneath the metal directly into the wood decking and soffit system.

### Categorization
**[Repair Quote Required]** 
*(Coupled with an ongoing maintenance plan)*

### Recommendation & Rationale
The primary shingles appear to be in acceptable condition, aligning with the HOA board's initiative to prioritize targeted maintenance over full, premature replacements. However, the critical failure at the eave requires immediate invasive repairs. We must remove the lower courses of shingles, pull up the improperly layered metal, and correctly install new Ice & Water shield *over* the drip edge to ensure proper watershedding into the gutters. We also recommend upgrading the gutter fasteners to hidden brackets to prevent future fascia separation.

To maximize the remaining lifespan of the 70-90% of the roof that is currently in good condition, we highly recommend transitioning this building to a **[5-Year System Service Agreement]**. This ongoing maintenance plan **Includes reconditioning the asphalt and granules to extend adhesion and UV as well as watershedding.** This approach fulfills the HOA's directive to extend roof life while directly mitigating active water intrusion risks.
